Introduction to AI Ethics

AI ethics is the study of how to design, deploy, and use AI systems in ways that are fair, transparent, and respectful of human rights. Core principles include fairness, accountability, transparency, privacy, and safety. Ethics helps ensure AI benefits society while minimizing harms.

Bias in AI

Bias can enter AI systems through training data, model design, or deployment contexts. Common sources include unrepresentative datasets, historical inequalities reflected in data, and implicit assumptions made by designers. Mitigation strategies include auditing models for disparate impacts, using diverse datasets, testing in real-world scenarios, and involving multidisciplinary teams in development.

Privacy Concerns

Many AI systems rely on large amounts of personal data, raising concerns about consent, data protection, and re-identification. Best practices include collecting only necessary data, using anonymization techniques, following legal frameworks (like data protection laws), and being transparent with users about how their data is used.

Responsible AI Use

Responsible AI combines technical safeguards and organizational practices to reduce harm. Recommended practices are: perform impact assessments before deployment, document datasets and model decisions, monitor systems in production, provide clear channels for feedback and redress, and train teams on ethical principles.
