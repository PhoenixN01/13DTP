Introduction to AI

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the field of computer science focused on creating systems that can perform tasks that normally require human intelligence. AI technologies power everything from search engines and recommendation systems to medical diagnostics and autonomous vehicles. Today, AI is central to innovation across industries, helping automate repetitive work, uncover insights from data, and enable new capabilities.

History of AI

AI began as an academic discipline in the 1950s with early work on symbolic reasoning and problem solving. Over decades it went through highs and lows—periods of optimism and reduced funding—until the 2000s when larger datasets and improved computing power enabled breakthroughs in machine learning. The past decade has seen rapid progress in deep learning, natural language processing, and computer vision, bringing AI into everyday products.

Types of AI

Narrow (Weak) AI: Systems designed for specific tasks, such as language translation or image recognition.

General AI (AGI): A hypothetical system with flexible, human-like problem solving across a wide range of domains.

Superintelligent AI: A speculative level of intelligence that surpasses human performance across nearly all fields.

Applications of AI

  • Healthcare: Medical imaging, personalized treatment recommendations, and drug discovery.
  • Finance: Fraud detection, algorithmic trading, and credit scoring.
  • Education: Adaptive learning platforms, grading assistance, and tutoring chatbots.
  • Transportation: Route optimization and autonomous driving research.
  • Business: Customer service automation, demand forecasting, and process optimization.
